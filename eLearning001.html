1  Introduction

Welcome to the E-Learning for your course in Micro Economics.

In economics we want to derive a set of conclusions or theorems from a given set of assumptions via a process of reasoning1. For deriving such conclusions we need an approach to economic analysis. Here we will use economic models, in which the economist makes use of mathematical symbols in the statement of the problem. These models will help us in reasoning the conclusions (given our set of assumptions).

This E-Learning is intended to make you familiar with the mathematical theorems which are needed to understand microeconomic theory. During this E-Learning some mathematical theorems will be explained. But you do not have to be afraid, all mathematical theorems discussed here, should be already known from school, hence the E-Learning should just help you to bring back memories and maybe to view the things you learned in school in a different way. Furthermore all theorems will be presented in a non mathematical way, so that also students without the necessary background will understand the most important theorems.

At the end of this E-Learning you will have to answer some review questions. These questions should help you to test your mathematical skills. It is not necessary that all your solutions are correct. Wrong results indicate that you should repeat the mathematical backround from school once again (e.g. with help of this E-Learning).

After our first session the solutions will be provided here and you will have the possiblity to check your results! If you have no problems in answering the questions you should be able to understand all the models which will be discussed during the course. If you have still troubles in answering the questions after doing this E-Learning you should ask your lecturer for further literature to repeat the relevant mathematic theory.

Please note that the text represented in this E-Learning is based on the textbook Fundamental Methods of Mathematical Economics by Alpha C. Chiang and Kevin Wainwright, Fourth Edition, published by McGraw-Hill/Irwin, ISBN 007-123823-9. The following sections of the textbook will be covered by this E-Learning: 1.1, 2.1, 2.4-2.7, 6.1-6.3. As the (sub-)sections are named as in the textbook there will be no further references during the E-Learning.

Furthermore please note that this E-Learning is only intended for students of the branch tourism management who participate in the courses in Micro- and Macroeconomics. You are not allowed to share or forward the presented contents!
